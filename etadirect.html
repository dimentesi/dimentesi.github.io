What is ETAdirect?

This Enterprise solution is targeted at companies that deal with delivery or performing work on the client's premises.
By providing mobile workforce management and keeping constant connection with the end client, ETAdirect considerably increases efficiency and quality of the service that customers provide.
Particularly, system allows technicians and engineers of customers to receive tasks, report on their completion, as well as self-assign tasks in case there is no dispatch personnel.
ETAdirect helps dispatchers plan routes, create tasks for technicians as well as control their completion. Also, ETAdirect has a module, which predicts the workload and provides the means for its planning, namely graphs that display various parameters, integration with outside systems, automatic voice support, building reports, timesheet management, etc.
Product has about 20 components, each has a separate development cycle.

Core Principles

— Automated, predictive, and responsive to the needs of the moment
— Eliminate human error
— Gives customers the tools to manage their needs and provide feedback
— Gives the workforce the incentive and ability to work efficiently and enjoyably
— Beautiful and functional
— Saves customers money

Feature Highlights

— Automatic location based activity start and end
— Real time collaboration and communication
— In-the-field team management
— Business rules based workflows
— Multiple roles and role specific views
— Contextual alerts and content presentation
— Voice control for field employees

A Day in the Life
of ETAdirect

“A Day in the Life of ETAdirect” follows a field service employee and their supervisor as they use the system to get through a successful day. A broad range of scenarios demonstrate the core functionality and characteristics of the new ETAdirect.

The Morning Check-in

Mark, a supervisor at Sunrise Enterprise, starts his work day by signing into ETAdirect.

On the dashboard, he’s prompted to assign any unassigned activities for the day. He looks at his team’s schedule for the day, checks FSE status, and identifies any potential issues

Chris, an FSE at Sunrise Enterprise, also starts his work day by signing into ETAdirect.

The First Activity

Now that Chris has checked in for the day, he’s ready to start his first activity. He is provided with the best route to get to his activity, and alternate routes if unexpected delays come up on the way.

Laura, a Sunrise customer, receives an SMS from ETAdirect reminding her of her appointment time. She replies directly to the text message, confirming the details and adding that she’d like the FSE to call when he arrives.

When Chris arrives, he is automatically checked-in. Before leaving the van he checks the activity details and is alerted of the new customer request to call upon his arrival.

Laura gets Chris’ call and comes to greet him at the door. When Chris encounters a problem with the installation he looks up the FSE who performed the previous service call to the house. He calls his teammate, who gives him helpful information about the specific cable connection.

When Chris receives an automated reminder about the scheduled end of his activity, he lets the system know that he needs 15 more minutes. ETAdirect reroutes and the team’s schedules are updated to accommodate the delay.

As he closes the activity he leaves a note about the hyperactive dog for future visits. ETAdirect also asks him whether he liked the activity and he selects one of the stock answers. Having finished his check-out, Chris looks ahead to the next activity before heading out.

If Chris had forgotten to check-out of the activity, it would have automatically happened anyway as he left the activity location.

Managing the Team

Meanwhile, the supervisor manages his team from the road.
He receives a notification: one of his FSE’s is going home sick. ETAdirect is able to automatically reassign all but one group activity. Mark sees that his skills fit with the group activity so he assigns himself to it.

Chris gets a message that his new partner for the team activity will be his supervisor.

Picking Up an Activity

Chris, the FSE, has finished his scheduled activities, but has time for one more. He scans the list of non-scheduled activities. During breaks in Chris’ day, he can also check ETAdirect to see if any of his teammates need help with an activity.

Wrapping Up

When his day is done, Chris completes his daily wrap-up. ETAdirect slowly learns the preferences and strengths of each FSE, and can assign activities with these additional criteria, improving employee retention and customer satisfaction.

When Mark sees that his entire team has signed-out for the day, he also completes a daily wrap-up. He can review the performance of his team, overall, and then set goals and update the skill sets of his team members. ETAdirect also allows Mark to plan ahead, such as assessing future staffing needs.

Another successful day for Sunrise Enterprise.
